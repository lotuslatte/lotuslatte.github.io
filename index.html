About

At the crossroads of migration, climate & evidence.

I'm an economist from the Pontifical Catholic University of Peru, currently a Research Assistant at CIUP and collaborating with the Lancet Countdown Latin America, FLACSO, and IPA.

My work spans immobility in fragile contexts, climate-health economics, agricultural innovation, and the network strategies of microenterprises — using Latent Class Analysis, NLP, and geospatial methods.

Beyond research I'm a published poet, a cyclist, and a cinephile who believes rigorous evidence should always tell a human story.

Writing

Café sin Azúcar

A weekly newsletter where the things I care about collide: philosophy, poetry, data, film, Lima, and whatever won't let me sleep that week. This isn't a niche newsletter. It's a restless mind organized into four lines. One piece a week. No algorithm, no sugar, straight to your inbox.

I
Grounds & Verses
Poetry, fragments, reflections, urban chronicles. What happens when I walk through Lima with my eyes open and a notebook in my pocket. Sometimes in Spanish, sometimes in English — depends on who broke my heart and in what language.
II
The Long Brew
Essays on philosophers, concepts, and ideas I can't stop thinking about. Byung-Chul Han and Lima's burnout. Spinoza and affect. Foucault and water. Philosophy without jargon, connected to life.
III
Caffeine Dreams
Philosophical analysis of film, books, and the stories that stay with you. Can a machine suffer? Who decides who deserves to be human? The questions fiction asks when reality doesn't dare.
IV
Dato Amargo
Data-driven essays on climate, migration, economics, and politics. A hard number opens it, a human story holds it, an argument closes it. What I learned walking Andean communities and reading papers at 3am. Always in Spanish.
